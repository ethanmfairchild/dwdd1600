Most Famous Signers

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the 3rd President of the United States, oversaw the Louisiana Purchase, and founded the University of Virginia.
John Adams
Vocal advocate for independence during the Continental Congress. Became the 2nd President of the United States and played a key diplomatic role in securing European support during the Revolutionary War.
Benjamin Franklin
Assisted in editing the Declaration and was a key member of the Continental Congress. Known as a statesman, inventor, and diplomat, Franklin negotiated the Treaty of Alliance with France and helped secure peace with Britain.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress. Famous for his bold, large signature, symbolizing his commitment to independence. His name became synonymous with signing documents.
Samuel Adams
A strong advocate for independence and key organizer of revolutionary activities like the Boston Tea Party. Played a significant role in mobilizing colonial resistance and helped draft the Articles of Confederation.
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the motion for independence in the Continental Congress. His resolution, the Lee Resolution, laid the foundation for drafting the Declaration.
